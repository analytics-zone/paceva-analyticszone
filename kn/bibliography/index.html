Bibliography & References

Paceva's analytical engine is built on validated, peer-reviewed sports science models. Here are the key publications behind our calculations.

1. W' Balance (Anaerobic Capacity Depletion)

The dynamic model for tracking anaerobic work capacity remaining above Critical Power is derived from:

  • Skiba, P. F., et al. (2012). "Modeling the expenditure and reconstitution of work capacity above critical power." Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 44(8), 1526-1532.
  • Skiba, P. F., et al. (2014). "Validation of a novel model for providing real-time feedback on anaerobic capacity." International Journal of Sports Physiology and Performance, 9(6), 936-942.

2. Heart Rate Variability (HRV) & Autonomic Recovery

The interpretation of rMSSD and autonomic nervous system status for athletic recovery is based on:

  • Plews, D. J., et al. (2013). "Evaluating training adaptation with heart-rate variability: a review." International Journal of Sports Physiology and Performance, 8(2), 107-112.
  • Kiviniemi, A. M., et al. (2007). "Daily exercise prescription on the basis of HR variability." International Journal of Sports Medicine, 28(10), 835-842.

3. Critical Swim Speed (CSS)

Pacing zones and Critical Speed modeling for swimming aerobic threshold development:

  • Wakayoshi, K., et al. (1992). "Determination and validity of critical velocity as an index of swimming performance in the competitive swimmer." European Journal of Applied Physiology and Occupational Physiology, 64(2), 153-157.

4. Gait Analysis & Run Efficiency

Symmetry, ground contact time, and vertical oscillation implications for running economy:

  • Moore, I. S. (2016). "Is there an economical running gait? The links between biomechanics, running economy, and active injury prevention." Sports Medicine, 46(6), 793-807.
